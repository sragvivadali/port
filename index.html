Solar / Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicle

Objective: The objective was to design, create, and test a vehicular prototype that is fueled using a solar or hydrogen fuel cell as its power source while maximizing force and speed.


In this project, my team and I needed to create a functioning vechile using a solar and hydrogen fuel cell. During this project, my main contribution was to help build the mechanism. Since there were many awards for the designs, one being a creative award, I explained that we should build a motorcycle shape. This is due to a motorcycle having a relatively light shape, making it easily move faster. In this project, I was the lead builder as for I explained to my teammates the shape and structure of the vehicle. Going through the design procress numerous times, we finally created a functioning vehicle. This project was difficult due to voltage and current outputted by the hydrogen cells. These cells give so little voltage, it is difficult for it to move a "heavy" vehicle. At the end, I also created a 2D simulation on the parts of the vehicle to test and present the values similar to our prototype. I did this by using a GUI software called Netlogo and I simulated the vehicle in its many configurations. I also worked to finalize the documentation.

Paper Rockets Design

Objective: The objective was to design, create, test, and conclude on a specific projectile using paper and tape to travel the longest distance.


In this project, my team and I needed to create a projectile that can travel a large distance. During this project, my main contribution was to create two designs of projectiles. For this project, we needed to create a projectile that can travel the farthest. This distance was tracked vertically. For my projectile, I decided to create a rocket. This rocket was created using printer paper and transparent packaging tape. When we were testing the rockets, I decided that 45 degrees was the most efficient angle to launch the rocket and it traveled 31 yards in 10 seconds at 60psi. This was due to the wind catching the rocket and caused it to slow down faster. Seeing my mistakes, I created another rocket similar to the previous rocket. I created the body using two papers, cardstock and printer paper, decided to decrease the fins size so that it doesn't catch onto the wind. I also wrapped the entire rocket with duct tape and increase the compression of the rocket. This rocket traveled 88 yards in approximately 3 seconds. Another aspect of this project is creating a 2d simulation using NetLogo. Using Aerodynamic equations, I was able to create a simulation for a rocket launch adding factors such as wind and initial velocity. This helped me find the best angle of launch, testing them in the virtual simulation. I also worked to finalize the documentation.

During my time in the engineering program, I have met several industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. These guest speakers helped shape my perspectives on engineering principles I am interested in.


Continue Reading From Here:

One engineering field I am interested in is Civil Engineering. Emma Schoental, a civil engineer, came to our school as a guest speaker on this topic. She explained the multiple types of civil engineers, as well as types of projects that they work on. This guest speaker showed me the daily life of civil engineers and I was inspired by the process involved in a project and the use of simulation and AutoDesk, showing the need for numerous engineering principles involved in a single project. This guest speaker showed me the background of one of the engineering fields I am interested in and also showed how the daily life of a civil engineer is like. Another guest speaker was Maynard Holiday, a member of the team involved in the containment of the Chernobyl Powerplant. He discussed the methods of using robotics to help diagnose the powerplant as well as the methods used to contain the radiation from the explosion. As a member of the Dublin High VEX Robotics team, one of my ambitions is to use my knowledge of robotics to help benefit the world. As someone who did it, Mr. Holiday helped me understand how they used robotics to accomplish something that a human could not do. With this, it helped me truly understand the robotics field, causing my interest to skyrocket.


One field trip that I attended is the Jet Patriot Flight Hanger. There, we were taught about the process of rocket launching and the necessity to launch a man onto the moon by the many volunteers of the Patriots Jet Team Foundation. This inspired me about the ideas that can aid us with space travel. It also opened my eyes as to the difficult process in the development and process needed to reach Mars. This gave me a great perspective of aerodynamics and also showed that it could be part of my future career. Another field trip that I went to was the Lawernce Nation Laboratory National Ignition Facility (NIF). There, we were able to see the process of conducting experiments in the laboratory. They explained that they initially run many simulations before initializing the laser. This interested me because of my enthusiasm for computer sciences. By using the laws of fusion and light equations, the group can predict the output and how long a reaction will occur during the test. With running the simulation, they will not be able to get precise data before running the test, to ensure the safety of the scientist. This use of simulations helped show me a new method of using computer science compared to the known methods of web development and corporate data management.
